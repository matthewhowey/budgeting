1. How it works

You can apply for a Budgeting Loan to help pay for;

  • furniture or household equipment
  • clothing or footwear
  • rent in advance if you find a new place to live
  • removal expenses when you move home
  • travelling expenses
  • things to help you look for or start work
  • improving, maintaining or securing your home
  • maternity or funeral expenses
  • repaying hire purchases or ther debts you took out to pay for any of the above

A Budgeting Loan is interest-free so you only pay back what you borrow. The smallest amount you can borrow is Â£100. Loans are usually paid into your account, eg a bank account.

You normally have to repay the loan within 104 weeks. The repayments will come from your benefits. You'll have tp agree another way to repay if you stop getting benefits.

If you get Universal Credit, apply for a Budgeting Advance from your loacal jobcentre Plus instead
