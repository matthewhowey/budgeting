1. How it works

You can apply for a Budgeting Loan to help pay for:

  • furniture or household items
  • clothing or footwear
  • rent in advance
  • costs linked to moving house
  • maintenance, security or improvements to your home
  • travelling costs within the UK
  • costs linked to getting a new job
  • maternity costs
  • funeral costs
  • repaying hire purchase loans
  • repaying loans taken for the above items

A Budgeting Loan is interest-free so you only pay back what you borrow. The smallest amount you can borrow is Â£100. Loans are usually paid into your account, eg a bank account.

You normally have to repay the loan within 104 weeks. The repayments will come from your benefits. You'll have to agree another way to repay if you stop getting benefits.

If you get Universal Credit, apply for a budgeting advance from your local Jobcentre Plus instead.
