Budgeting Loan application unsuccessful

Your application

Dear Jane Smith,

On 25 March 2016 you applied for a Budgeting Loan of £500. After reviewing your application, we’re unable to offer you a Budgeting Loan at this time.

The reason that your application has been unsuccessful is because you or your partner don’t fully meet the full 4 eligibility points below:

  • you must not owe the Social Fund more than £1,500 for Crisis Loans and Budgeting Loans
  • you must have been getting Income Support, income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance, income-related Employment and Support Allowance or Pension Credit for the past 6 months (and you must still be getting one of these benefits when you apply)
  • you must need £100 or more
  • you must not be involved in industrial action (eg a strike, walkout or lockout)

If you’re unhappy with this decision

You can ask for a Budgeting Loan decision to be reviewed. To do this, you must write to Jobcentre Plus before 22 April 2016, explaining why you think it was wrong.

Find your nearest Jobcentre Plus

If you still think the decision was wrong after the review, you can ask for a further review by the office of the Independent Case Examiner. This review should take no longer than 21 working days.

Other help you can get

You may be able to get other kinds of support, including:

  • help from your local council and Jobcentre Plus
  • the Discretionary Assistance Fund in Wales
  • a Crisis Loan or Community Care Grant in Northern Ireland or Scotland
